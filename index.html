Global Water Stress: Current and Future

The lack of sufficient available water resources to meet the demands of water usage already affects every continent and around 2.8 billion people around the world at least one month out of every year. According to the Food and Agriculture Organization of United Nations, by 2025, 1.8 billion people will experience absolute water scarcity, and 2/3 of the world will be living under water-stressed conditions. This visualization focuses on the water stress level in every country from 1960 to 2017, as well as predicting the future water stress level up to 2040, by looking at the ratio between the water withdrawal and the total available water in each nation annually. The predications for the future are based on three categories: Pessimistic, Optimistic, and Business as Usual.
